# **Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students**



UPI Tip 1: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. You only need your PIN to send money or check your balance.



UPI Tip 2: Double-check the receiver's name. Always verify the registered name that appears on your UPI app before hitting send, especially when scanning QR codes at local campus shops.



Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never make fee payments or log into your bank account while connected to open, unencrypted campus or cafe Wi-Fi.



Enable 2FA on Everything: Turn on Two-Factor Authentication for your college email, GitHub, and all social media accounts.



Verify "Urgent" Requests: If a friend or family member asks for emergency money on WhatsApp, call them directly to confirm it is actually them.



Lock Down Social Media: Keep your accounts private so scammers cannot steal your photos or map out your friend circles.



Beware of Phishing Links: Do not click on links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages offering free gifts, jobs, or scholarships.



Update Your Devices: Keep your phone's OS and your banking apps updated to the latest versions to patch security vulnerabilities.



### 🚨 What to do if you are scammed:

Immediately report the incident to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930.
