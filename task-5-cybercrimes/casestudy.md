# **Case Study: The "Urgent Hostel Fee Refund" UPI Fraud**



What the crime is: UPI/Online Payment Fraud involves scammers tricking victims into authorizing money transfers using the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) system by exploiting fear, greed, or urgency.



Who is usually targeted: College students in India are frequent targets. They are often managing their own finances for the first time, paying semester fees, and looking for scholarships.



How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):

1\. A first-year student receives an official-looking SMS claiming their recent university hostel fee payment failed, but a refund of ₹15,000 has been initiated.

2\. The message contains a link that directs the student to a fake, but highly convincing, portal that asks for their UPI ID to "deposit" the refunded amount.

3\. Shortly after entering the ID, the student receives a payment request on their Google Pay or PhonePe app.

4\. The scammer calls the student, pretending to be a bank or university official, and urgently tells them they must "Enter their UPI PIN to accept the refund" before the link expires in two minutes.

5\. Panicking and believing they are receiving their money back, the student enters their secret PIN.



The Consequences: Because a UPI PIN is only ever used to deduct money, entering the PIN instantly transfers ₹15,000 out of the student's bank account to the scammer. The student loses their money, faces immense stress, and often struggles to recover the funds since the transaction was technically authorized by them.
